Title
Pogo and Air Combat Prompt

Purpose
Deepen Shadow Shift's aerial combat by improving downslash/pogo behavior and making airborne attack decisions more expressive and reliable.

Why This Matters for V2
Air combat turns traversal into combat and combat into traversal. When pogo is strong and readable, the world design possibilities expand dramatically.

Detailed Codex Instructions
Context:
The game already has a downslash/pogo-style mechanic. V2 should make it cleaner, more extensible, and better integrated with enemies, hazards, and room design.

Goal:
Refine pogo and air combat into a dependable system that supports combat mastery and traversal expression.

Implementation Steps:
1. Audit current aerial attack states, downslash detection, pogo bounce rules, valid targets, and hit feedback.
2. Separate pogo logic from general attack logic while keeping the systems compatible.
3. Make valid pogo surfaces explicit, such as enemies, spikes, breakables, and special world objects.
4. Improve bounce timing, bounce strength, and hazard grace rules so the mechanic feels fair and intentional.
5. Ensure the system is future-proof for elemental pogo interactions or special enemy counters.
6. Keep slash FX and hit feedback aligned with the mechanic.

Constraints:
- Preserve current melee architecture
- Avoid breaking normal aerial attacks
- Keep inputs simple and readable

Implementation Notes
- Consider a dedicated air attack profile table if attack tuning is getting too scattered.
- Make pogo success states easy to debug.
- Do not let pogo trivialize all hazards unless the design explicitly supports that.

Validation Checklist
- Downslash is easy to trigger intentionally
- Pogo bounce feels satisfying and consistent
- Enemies and hazards react correctly
- Air combat is more expressive than before
- Existing traversal remains valid or improves
