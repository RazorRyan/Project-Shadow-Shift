Title
Elite Enemy Design Prompt

Purpose
Introduce elite enemies in Shadow Shift that act as mini-encounters: tougher than standard enemies, lighter than bosses, and capable of showcasing the game's deeper combat systems.

Why This Matters for V2
Elites create memorable spikes in tension and skill checks. They are also excellent bridges between basic enemies and full bosses.

Detailed Codex Instructions
Context:
The game already includes basic enemies and at least one boss. V2 should add a middle tier that can anchor combat rooms and reward mastery.

Goal:
Design and implement one or more elite enemy templates using shared AI, reaction, and combat systems.

Implementation Steps:
1. Define what makes an elite in Shadow Shift:
   - more layered move set
   - stronger punish/reward loop
   - clearer phase or aggression shift
   - stronger reactions and resistances
2. Choose one existing enemy family to elevate or create a new elite archetype.
3. Implement elite-specific attack logic, recovery windows, and telegraphs.
4. Add stronger reward signals on defeat, such as checkpoints, pickups, or route opening.
5. Place elites in a meaningful room context.

Constraints:
- Do not make elites into full boss fights
- Keep encounters readable
- Reuse shared systems and data where possible

Implementation Notes
- Elites should teach boss-like reading in a smaller package.
- Avoid pure health sponge design.
- Make their identity obvious from silhouette, cadence, and arena behavior.

Validation Checklist
- Elite enemies feel meaningfully above standard enemies
- Encounters remain fair and learnable
- The elite uses shared systems cleanly
- Reward/pacing payoff feels appropriate
- The game gains stronger mid-tier combat variety
